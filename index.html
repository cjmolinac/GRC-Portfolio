Decision Rationale

Context
TechStart Inc. completed a cloud migration and required a formal post-migration risk assessment to validate that critical information security controls were properly implemented and operating effectively across key assets, ensuring compliance with ISO 27001 requirements.
Key Decisions
Security tools for backup immutability (S3 Object Lock) and access analysis (AWS IAM Access Analyzer) were selected from the cloud provider's native catalog. Decision Rationale

Context
FinSecure Banking required integration with PaySecure Solutions, a third-party payment processor with direct access to customer cardholder data. Given the sensitivity of the data involved and OCC regulatory obligations, a formal third-party risk assessment was required to evaluate PaySecure's security posture, verify PCI DSS and SOC 2 compliance, and ensure cardholder data protection prior to contract execution.
Key Decisions
PCI DSS and SOC 2 were selected as the primary evaluation frameworks, as they directly govern payment card data security and third-party service provider attestation requirements. While ISO 27001 controls were considered as an alternative framework, PCI DSS requirements were prioritized as they are mandated by the payment card industry regulators and directly applicable to PaySecure's operating environment.
Outcomes
FinSecure established minimum security and compliance requirements for third-party payment processor integration, ensuring cardholder data protection aligned with PCI DSS and OCC regulations. The assessment also prompted an internal review of FinSecure's own compliance controls and contract management practices, resulting in strengthened vendor contract terms including specific regulatory obligations, penalty clauses, and remediation timelines for future third-party engagements.
